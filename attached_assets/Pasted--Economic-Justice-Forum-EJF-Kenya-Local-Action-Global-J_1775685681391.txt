 
Economic Justice Forum (EJF) – Kenya

Local Action, Global Justice
 
Executive Summary
The Economic Justice Forum (EJF) is Kenya’s premier grassroots-to-global advocacy platform advancing economic, climate, digital, and resource justice. Founded to empower communities that host and protect natural resources yet receive little direct benefit from their wealth, EJF transforms historical exclusion into participatory governance, equitable resource management, and sustainable local development.
EJF operates a vertically and horizontally integrated justice model, leveraging Kenya’s constitutional devolution framework to secure tangible economic, land, environmental, and cultural rights. Through grassroots action, evidence-based research, policy advocacy, and global engagement, the Forum ensures constitutional mandates—such as equitable resource benefit-sharing—are implemented, monitored, and expanded at local, national, and international levels.
EJF’s achievements in Taita Taveta County demonstrate its ability to convert legal frameworks into real-world community empowerment, including the enactment of the Taita Taveta Mining Act (2016), resolution of historical land disputes, and climate finance innovations such as Debt-for-Climate Swaps. Forward-looking initiatives, including the Climate Justice and Innovation AI Hub—100% solar-powered and AI-driven—position EJF at the forefront of ethical technology, participatory governance, climate resilience, and cultural preservation in East Africa.
 
Vision
A just, equitable, sustainable, and digitally inclusive Kenya where every person enjoys dignity, opportunity, and prosperity, and where resource-hosting communities are empowered to claim their rightful share of wealth, decision-making, and recognition of their cultural heritage.
 
Mission
To advance economic, climate, resource, social, and digital justice by empowering communities, promoting systemic reforms, ensuring transparent and fair management of resources, preserving cultural identity, and fostering sustainable development locally, nationally, and globally.
 
Strategic Pillars
1. Economic, Fiscal & Debt Justice
•	Ensure fair taxation, budget transparency, responsible debt management, and sustainable economic empowerment.
•	Promote participatory budgeting, citizen scorecards, and social audits to monitor resource allocation.
•	Strengthen county and national legislative frameworks for economic accountability.
•	Advocate for Debt-for-Climate Swaps and direct climate finance to grassroots communities, ensuring a People’s Climate Dividend reaches those protecting natural resources.
2. Climate & Resource Justice
•	Guarantee equitable community benefits from natural resources while building climate resilience.
•	Lobby for Natural Resource Benefit-Sharing laws at national and UN levels.
•	Promote renewable energy, circular economy, eco-tourism, sustainable forestry, and climate-smart livelihoods.
•	Build local capacity in climate-smart agriculture, ecosystem restoration, and AI-powered resource monitoring.
•	Recognize and integrate cultural practices as a driver of sustainable resource stewardship and economic empowerment.
3. Democracy, Digital Justice, Leadership & Devolution
•	Strengthen ethical leadership, civic engagement, digital inclusion, and efficient county governance.
•	Conduct comprehensive constitutional audits on devolution and governance reform.
•	Integrate ethical AI and digital tools to monitor resource allocation and ensure algorithmic fairness.
•	Operate a Leadership Academy for youth and women, fostering civic education, leadership, and community empowerment.
4. Global Advocacy & Governance Reform
•	Influence international policies and financial systems to support inclusive economic, climate, and resource justice.
•	Advocate for UN Natural Resource Benefit-Sharing Frameworks and Conventions.
•	Engage with global financial institutions (IMF, World Bank, WTO) to promote transparency, fairness, and equitable benefit-sharing.
•	Amplify African grassroots voices in global policy-making, including post-COP30 climate strategies.
 
Key Achievements
•	Taita Taveta Mining Act (2016): Institutionalized community economic empowerment and revenue-sharing.
•	Land Rights Successes: Returned Mkuki Ranch, Manga Community Land, and Singila Majengo plots, correcting historical injustices.
•	Climate & Biodiversity Leadership: Partnered with the Global Carbon Council, planted 20,000 trees, and inducted 20,000 Tree Champions.
•	Digital Innovation: Conceptualized the Climate Justice & Innovation AI Hub for scalable algorithmic accountability and participatory governance.
•	Policy Influence: Contributed to county-level climate legislation and ongoing advocacy for UN frameworks on resource benefit-sharing.
 
Geographic Focus
•	Coastal regions (Taita Taveta, Kilifi, Kwale, Lamu): Blue economy, marine resources, land rights, cultural stewardship.
•	Arid & Semi-Arid Regions (Turkana, Isiolo, Samburu): Climate resilience, water management, pastoralist livelihoods.
•	Resource-Rich Counties (Kajiado, Laikipia, Nyandarua): Mining, forestry, biodiversity, equitable benefit-sharing.
•	Urban Centers (Nairobi, Mombasa): Digital inclusion, innovation, civic engagement, and economic opportunity.
 
Strategic Partners, Donors & Development Partners
Local: County governments, County Assemblies, Parliament, IEBC, civil society organizations, grassroots communities, media, religious institutions, academia, political parties, LSK, County Assemblies Forum, Council of Governors.
Regional: African Union, regional economic communities, NGOs, civil society networks, Resource Justice Network.
Global & International Foundations: UN agencies, OECD, IMF, World Bank, WTO, European Union, GIZ, Open Society Foundations, National Endowment for Democracy, IRI, NDI, philanthropic networks, research institutions, and universities.
 
Core Approaches
•	Human rights and economic justice-centered advocacy and lobbying.
•	Grassroots and leadership capacity building.
•	Mandatory, structured, sector-based public participation.
•	Evidence-based research, knowledge management, and innovation integration.
•	Ethical AI and technology deployment for transparency, accountability, and governance.
 
Priority Projects (2026–2028)
1.	Climate Justice Innovation Hub (CJIH): Solar-powered, AI-driven innovation and incubation hub for climate, resource, and digital justice.
2.	Natural Resource Benefit-Sharing & Policy Advocacy: National law development and UN integration.
3.	Capacity Building, Governance & Institutional Strengthening: County and parliamentary oversight, leadership, and civic engagement.
4.	Comprehensive Constitutional Audit on Devolution: Addressing administrative, structural, and legal bottlenecks.
5.	Grassroots Economic Empowerment Programs: Targeted support for communities hosting natural resources and facing climate impacts.
